Switchpoint Rental Assistance Programs

Switchpoint offers rental assistance programs in both Washington County and Tooele County. Each program is designed to support individuals and families experiencing housing instability, with specific eligibility requirements and services based on program guidelines.

Washington County Programs

Eligible residents may qualify for one or more of the following programs:

Permanent Supportive Housing (PSH)

  • Must have a documented disability
  • Must meet the definition of chronic homelessness
  • Includes long-term services and program participation

Rapid Rehousing (RRH)

  • Must currently be experiencing homelessness
  • Provides short-term rental assistance and case management
  • Helps individuals and families transition quickly into stable housing

TANF (Temporary Assistance for Needy Families)

  • Must have at least one dependent child under age 18
  • Provides temporary rental assistance for families in financial crisis
  • Includes short-term services and program requirements

Tooele County Program

HOME‑ARP

  • Must be homeless or at risk of homelessness
  • Must meet income eligibility criteria
  • Includes supportive services and program participation

Program Eligibility

Each program has unique qualifications and participation requirements. Eligibility is determined through a formal application and assessment process. If you believe you may qualify, please visit the program pages to learn more.
