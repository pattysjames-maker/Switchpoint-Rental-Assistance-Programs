TANF Rental Assistance

TANF Rental Assistance provides short‑term housing support to families with children who are experiencing financial hardship or housing instability. This program helps prevent eviction, supports rapid stabilization, and connects families with resources that promote long‑term independence.

What TANF Rental Assistance Provides

Short‑term rental support

  • Assistance with rent to prevent eviction or support a new housing placement.
  • Short‑term, solution‑focused support based on household need.

Housing stability support

  • Case management and service coordination
  • Employment and income support through community resources
  • Referrals to childcare, mental health services, or other supports

Tenant‑based housing

  • Families reside in standard community housing such as apartments, townhomes, or rental homes.
  • Units must comply with local housing standards.

Who Qualifies for TANF Rental Assistance

Eligibility varies by household circumstances but must meet all general criteria below.

1. Families With Children

  • Household must include at least one dependent child under age 18, or
  • A pregnant individual in the third trimester.

2. Income Eligibility

  • Household income must fall within Utah TANF income guidelines (generally 200% of the federal poverty level).
  • Income limits adjust based on household size.
  • Some households are categorically eligible, including those receiving:
    • SNAP (Food Stamps)
    • Childcare Assistance
    • Medicaid or CHIP
    • WIC benefits

3. Housing Need

  • Risk of eviction
  • Past‑due rent
  • Need for assistance obtaining or moving into new housing
  • Other urgent housing‑related financial needs

Additional Support Services

TANF‑eligible households may also receive supportive services such as:

  • Case management
  • Employment and job training assistance
  • Financial literacy and budgeting support
  • Connections to childcare or community programs
