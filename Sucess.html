Switchpoint Rental Assistance Programs Success Stories

Below are four client success stories.  Each of these clients has received a long-term housing assistance grant. Each story is true and the names have been replaces with gender and or a random letter. 

Story 1

P & D are a married couple in their 60's who receive social security benefits. They became homeless after an eviction that occurred when they helped a family member going through a crisis. At that time, they entered into the Switchpoint Shelter and were able to qualify for long-term housing assistance through a Grant administered by the Switchpoint Housing Team. The couple has been through a lot of hardships, including most recently, both battling major medical issues. Regardless of all they have gone through, they have remained optimistic about their future and credit the Grant and the support provided by the Shelter Team and their Switchpoint Housing Case Manager for the stability they now have in their lives. We at Switchpoint credit their willingness to make changes and their commitment to each other and to building a stable life for themselves.

    Some of the activities that highlight this couple's attitude to life as well as their resilience include:
    • This past Thanksgiving P&D volunteered at the local (St. George) Community Thanksgiving Dinner, wishing to give back to those less fortunate
    • MD enjoys and participates in her community through her church
    • They both speak with great optimism about their future beyond the Grant
    • Whilst receiving rental assistance through the Grant, the couple were able to build up savings and an Emergency Fund, so they can remain financially self-sufficient, no matter what life brings

The Switchpoint team is in awe of this couple and their commitment to always moving forward with positivity.

Story 2

This family of four had been through challenging times for several years. Dad was homeless on and off for more than 2 years, ending most recently in residential treatment for alcohol use and Mom and the two children had been living in what is formally known as a place not meant for habitation — in this case, literally, a broken down building with no electricity or running water. With the support of the Switchpoint Shelter and Housing Teams (through a long-term housing assistance Grant) this family was able to come together and to go beyond maintaining, to thriving. With the guidance of their Housing Case Manager, the family reunited in their own, safe, permanent, rental living space. With parenting education assistance, the parents were able to come together to co-parent their children with positivity and love, whilst building financial stability for their family. In short, Mom & Dad dedicated themselves to re-building a positive, loving family environment, pursuing their goals with tenacity and willingness.

    Some of the many accomplishments the family-members achieved, include:
    • Dad working with DWS to secure and maintain eligible part-time work
    • Dad continuing to work hard on his SSDI application process
    • Dad maintaining his sobriety and consistently attending to all requirements of his probation
    • Mom starting employment at $9/Hr and then securing better employment at $16/Hr with Digby's, where she is extremely grateful for all the employment benefits that others may take for granted including paid time off, healthcare benefits and a 401K plan.  Mom states, quite emotionally that she never imagined herself in a job that provides so much support — she LOVES her work and her employer.
    • Whilst receiving rental assistance through the Housing Grant, Mom and Dad paid off debts, including court fines and fees, some long-owed
    • Mom was successful in getting her Drivers License back
    • The family was able to "graduate" from an often-broken-down vehicle, to a more reliable car
    • The now-18 year-old son, who has some permanent health struggles and receives SSDI, has dedicated himself to working with Vocational Rehabilitation to potentially be able to obtain employment of his own *His 15 year-old sister is on-track to graduate High School and has been consistently attending therapy appointments

This family has been very vocal in their appreciation of the Switchpoint team; in truth, we are equally appreciative of the opportunity to support this amazing family in all their efforts. They, together, have done all the hard work and we are so proud to have been a part of their journey.

Story 3

S had been homeless throughout her addiction; in survival mode, alone and separated from her beloved child.  She began her slow and steady journey to healing, self-reliance and happiness when she entered into the Switchpoint Shelter and continued her positive trajectory with the support of long-term housing assistance, and a dedicated partner in her Switchpoint Housing Case Manager.  S' focus, always, has been on not just reuniting with her now-teenager, but re-building a trusting, close relationship.  She has succeeded!

  • S threw herself into her recovery, not just maintaining, but continuing to draw strength and support from her NA Community each and every day
  • She obtained employment with a local grocery store, and then moved from strength to strength in her role — her stable employment has been marked by promotions and pay raises and illustrates how much her employer values all that S brings to her workplace
  • Her child, C, was able to come back to live with S — in a permanent home that she worked hard to obtain and even harder to maintain
  • S spends ALL her spare time continuing to nurture her relationship with C; she is  fiercely dedicated to moving beyond mere reparation with her child, to creating a lasting relationship that breaks what could have been a generational cycle of addiction and/or homelessness
  • S credits the positive parenting skills she learned and the positive communication skills she and C both learned through Switchpoint as the reasons for their wonderful relationship today.  Her Switchpoint Team credits S and C; they are the ones who committed themselves to the considerable care and work it takes to build a loving bond after family trauma

      • They love to prepare meals and eat together
  • They have fun hiking and camping  together
  • C now attends Utah Arts Academy and is thriving in the creative and rigorous academic environment
  • TS just taught C to drive.  A feat neither could even have imagined a year ago
  • Recently, they spent a weekend baking banana bread together and then hand-delivered the bread as Thank You gifts to the individuals and organizations that supported their journey.

S just informed her Switchpoint Housing Case Manager that she no longer needed the Housing Grant support, stating that she and C are now financially stable and that she is able to maintain their home and their life without additional support.  Tellingly, she wanted Switchpoint to use the Grant to help a new family or individual — somebody at the beginning of their own journey to "normal".  It has been a gift to work alongside S&C and to witness their strength, their love and support of each other and their success.

Story 4

V is a survivor of sex-trafficking. V is a survivor of Domestic Violence. V has survived years of homelessness - "living" in the underground tunnels far beneath the glamour of Las Vegas, with hundreds & hundreds of other homeless — what are sometimes referred to as " the Las Vegas Mole People". V is a Warrior — she fights every day for herself, for her son, for her family and for survivors of sex-trafficking. V began her healing journey when she made the decision to leave Las Vegas, her abusers, her traffickers and all the people who held her down and held her back. She decided to return home to St George, where she first began to access help and support through the local DV Shelter. By the time, weeks later, she was chosen for a long-term housing Grant through Switchpoint, V had already begun working on replacing her survival methods (negative coping mechanisms) with positive, healthy alternatives, and on recovering from years of horrific trauma. She had already begun working full-time as a food server. She had already begun the lengthy court process to reunite with her teenage son.


In her own, permanent, safe and stable housing, obtained with the assistance of the Grant, V continued to fight every day to improve her life and to create a life that is safe and filled with hope.

  • She began to make reparation for the damage she'd caused herself, her family and the community — working through her legal challenges, paying off court fines and doing everything humanly possible to begin the slow process of re-building family relationships with the people she'd alienated during her struggles and her abuse
  • She fought tirelessly to do everything the Legal System requires of her to be able to welcome her son back into her life and into her home.  She succeeded
  • With her son back in her life, she sought and obtained more stable employment that enabled her to spend evenings and weekends with her son
  • V fought for her own trauma recovery through consistent attendance and active participation in a Recovery Group for sex-trafficked individuals

      • V has transformed her life, her son's life, her relationship with her family and now, the lives of others:
  • She and her son, now 17yrs old, have a close and trusting relationship
  • Her son is a proud member of his HS Marching Band and enjoys his involvement in the school's ROTC Program
  • V spends joyous times babysitting her adult daughter's 2 infants; she is beyond grateful for the trust her daughter now places in her
  • Not content with working on her own trauma recovery and on building her own resilience and healthy life-skills, V is dedicated to helping other victims of sex-trafficking in their healing journey and path beyond survival to a healthy, abuse-free life.  As a Volunteer, V fights alongside other victims to help them create a better life for themselves and become survivors.

V is a Warrior.  V is an Inspiration to her Switchpoint Team, and to all who have the good fortune to get to know her.
