HomeArp Rental Assistance

Homearp can provide rental assistance for housed and homeless clients.

HomeArp is a short‑term housing assistance program designed to help individuals and families quickly exit homelessness, move into permanent housing and to aviod eviction. HomeArp focuses on stability, income growth, and overcoming barriers to maintaining housing. It is intended for people who can sustain housing after receiving temporary financial and supportive services.

What HomeArp Rapid Rehousing Provides

Short‑term rental assistance

  • Assistance typically lasts up to 12 months, with the average around 3–6 months.
  • Participants sign a standard lease and may remain housed after assistance ends.

Tenant‑based housing

  • Participants locate housing in the community, including apartments, scattered‑site units, or homes.
  • Units must meet HUD guidelines and Fair Market Rent (FMR) limits.

Who Qualifies for RRH

RRH is intended for individuals and families experiencing homelessness who have relatively low barriers to long‑term housing stability.

Must be experiencing homelessness

HUD‑aligned homelessness includes individuals who are:

  • Living in an emergency shelter
  • Staying in a place not meant for human habitation (car, street, camp, etc.)
  • Fleeing or attempting to flee domestic violence without safe housing options

Who Qualifies for HP

HomeArp Homeless Prevection

  • Risk of eviction
  • Past‑due rent
  • Need for assistance obtaining or moving into new housing
  • Other urgent housing‑related financial needs

Both Programs require:

Must have low to moderate housing barriers

HOmeArp is intended for individuals who can maintain stable housing with short‑term assistance.

  • Some income or ability to obtain income
  • Ability to meet basic lease requirements
  • No need for intensive long‑term supportive services

Must complete housing eligibility requirements

  • Willingness to participate in case management
  • Referral and documentation through Coordinated Entry (CE)

Required Documentation

  • Coordinated Entry enrollment through HMIS (if Homeless)
  • Verification from case management or referring provider
  • Eviction notice if housed

Supportive Services

HomeArp includes short‑term supportive services to help households stabilize, such as:

  • Case management
  • Employment and income support
  • Mental and behavioral health connections
  • Healthcare navigation
  • Access to community resources

Why rental assistance Matters

HomeArp is a effective short‑term interventions for many households because it:

  • Helps individuals and families exit homelessness quickly
  • Reduces shelter stays and system strain
  • Improves stability through income and resource support
  • Promotes long‑term independence
  • Keeps clients in their homes
